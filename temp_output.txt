href="#1.18">Samples</a></p>\n\t\n<a name="1.1" id="1.1"></a>\n<div class="headerwrap">\n<h2>1.1&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; Fundamentals</h2>\n</div>\n\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.1</div>\n\t<div class="sub-section-copy"><em>Braille Formats: Principles of Print-to-Braille Transcription, 2016</em> (called <em>guidelines</em> in the following sections) provides a foundation for transcribing content in an accurate and consistent manner. This document is a set of guidelines that should be used to make structuring decisions. As print is evolving constantly, it is neither possible nor practical to provide directives for all potential transcription problems. Today\xe2\x80\x99s transcriber will encounter unique print conventions and is expected to use experience and judgment in adapting the best practices provided in these guidelines when encountering situations not covered here.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.2</div>\n\t<div class="sub-section-copy">All decisions concerning the formation or placement of braille dots or symbols conform to the most recent editions of <em>The Rules of Unified English Braille (UEB)</em> (called UEB in the following sections) and to the rules and usages set forth in these guidelines. Exceptions are noted in <em>Formats</em>, \xc2\xa71.2, Technical and Specialized Materials of these guidelines and conform to those codes and any additional specialized braille rules or guidelines adopted by BANA following the adoption of this document.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.3</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Agencies may have specific requests for the format of print-to-braille material such as double-spacing, use of contracted or uncontracted braille, interpoint or single-sided embossing, inclusion or exclusion of some materials, and special formatting requests such as interlining.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.4</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">A transcriber does not edit text.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.5</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Print format cannot always be followed. If a particular format or font attribute cannot be represented in braille, the reader should be made aware of it through the use of a transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s note or other device.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.6</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Clarity of presentation always supersedes concerns about space. Space saving is not the primary consideration.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.7</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">A transcriber examines the print before beginning a transcription in order to structure the text consistently.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.8</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Text is transcribed as printed, with the wording and sequence retained. Marginal materials are placed where appropriate for their format.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.9</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Expendable materials follow the same formatting guidelines as permanent materials. An agency may require some modifications, e.g., retaining space for the student\xe2\x80\x99s name and date, leaving extra blank lines for writing answers, etc.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.10</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">A transcriber notifies the issuing agency when print cannot be transcribed. An example might be a book that is primarily pictures.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.11</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Print running headers and footers are ignored in braille. These often include the chapter or lesson title, the book title, etc.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t<div class="sub-section">1.1.12</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>A Braille Reader\xe2\x80\x99s Perspective.</strong> These guidelines are written so transcribers can produce the best possible braille for readers. In the past, BANA has issued rules that transcribers have been expected to follow even though many of them have never met a braille reader or seen braille read. We hope <em>A Braille Reader\xe2\x80\x99s Perspective</em> gives transcribers direction when interpreting the guidelines, especially in unusual situations.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t\n<a name="1.2" id="1.2"></a>\n\n<div class="headerwrap">\n<h2>1.2 &nbsp; &nbsp; Technical and Specialized Materials</h2>\n</div>\n\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.2.1</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Transcriptions of technical and specialized material <em>must</em> be undertaken <em>only</em> by transcribers who are trained in the use of the appropriate braille codes and guidelines, including all updates. See the BANA website at <a href="http://www.brailleauthority.org"  target="_blank">www.brailleauthority.org</a> for the most current code documents. There are guidelines for many different types of materials, including but not limited to, knit/crochet directions, music, tactile graphics, mathematics, and other technical materials. The website will provide additional guidelines as they are developed.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.2.2</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Graphics.</strong> Use the current <em>Guidelines and Standards for Tactile Graphics</em> when creating tactile graphics. The guidelines are available at <a href="http://www.brailleauthority.org/tg/index.html" target="_blank">http://www.brailleauthority.org/tg/index.html</a>.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.2.3</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Foreign Language.</strong> The use of contractions in foreign language within an English context has been approved by BANA. See <em>Formats,</em> \xc2\xa71.16, Foreign Material in English Context, for instructions on transcribing these types of materials.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.2.4</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Linguistics.</strong> The transcription of materials using specialized linguistics symbols of the International Phonetic Alphabet must be done in accordance with the IPA Braille guidelines found at <a href="http://www.brailleauthority.org/ipa/ipa-braille.html" target="_blank">http://www.brailleauthority.org/ipa/ipa-braille.html</a>.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.2.5</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Music Notation. </strong>The transcription of all music notation must be done in accordance with the provisions of the current <em>Music Braille Code</em>, available at <a href="http://www.brailleauthority.org/music/music.html" target="_blank">http://www.brailleauthority.org/music/music.html</a>.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.2.6</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Mathematics and Science.</strong> When transcribing technical content (e.g., mathematics, statistics, physics, or chemistry) that appears throughout the book, the materials should be transcribed according to UEB or Nemeth based on the specifications of the requesting entity. Consistency within a transcription is required. The following materials are available for reference:\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;<i>Nemeth Braille Code for Mathematics and Science Notation, 1972 Revision</i>, (and applicable updates). See the <i>Guidance for Transcription Using the Nemeth Code within UEB Context</i> available at <a href="http://www.brailleauthority.org/mathscience/math-science.html" target="_blank">http://www.brailleauthority.org/mathscience/math-science.html</a>.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. <i>&nbsp;Unified English Braille Guidelines for Technical Material</i> is a supplement to the UEB Rulebook that provides additional examples for technical materials transcribed using UEB. This reference is available at <a href="http://www.iceb.org/guidelines_for_technical_material_2014.pdf" target="_blank">http://www.iceb.org/guidelines_for_technical_material_2014.pdf</a>.</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\n<div class="headerwrap"><a name="1.3" id="1.3"></a>\n<h2>1.3 &nbsp; &nbsp; Partially Technical Works</h2>\n</div>\n\t\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.3.1</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Books with occasional mathematical or scientific terminology and notation are transcribed using UEB as the primary code. Symbols from other codes are listed on the Special Symbols page or in a transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s note before the text. Consistency within a transcription is required.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.3.2 </div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Text containing technical notation throughout the transcription requires notice of this usage on the Transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s Notes page. Samples:\n<p style=" width:500px;margin-left: 50px;margin-bottom:-18px;font-family:Cambria, Times, \'Times New Roman\', \'serif\'; text-indent: 20px;">Mathematical content is transcribed according to <em>The Nemeth Braille Code for Mathematics and Science Notation, 1972 Revision</em>, 2007\xe2\x80\x932016  updates and the <em>Guidance for Transcription Using the Nemeth Code within UEB Context.</em></p>\n<p style=" width:520px;margin-left: 50px;font-family:Cambria, Times, \'Times New Roman\', \'serif\'; text-indent: 20px;">Tactile graphics are produced according to the <em>Guidelines and Standards for Tactile Graphics, 2010.</em></p>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;Use of these codes requires knowledge and understanding of the code. It is the transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s responsibility to seek assistance from a code specialist to ensure an accurate transcription of the technical content.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;See <em>Formats,</em> \xc2\xa72.6, Transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s Notes Page, for detailed information about the Transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s Notes page.</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\n<div class="headerwrap"><a name="1.4" id="1.4"></a>\n<h2>1.4 &nbsp; &nbsp; Definitions, Format, and Terminology</h2>\n</div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.4.1</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">These guidelines contain definitions that identify print materials for which specific braille formats are provided. Regardless of print terminology, material in the braille edition is identified and transcribed according to the appropriate guideline definition. For example, columnar materials may be referred to as <em>lists, columns, tables, </em>or even <em>figures </em>in print. In braille, the material is identified first as fitting one of the specific definitions given for columnar material, lists, or tables and then transcribed accordingly.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.4.2</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><em>Blocked</em> refers to text alignment in which all lines begin at the same left margin.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.4.3</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Formatting patterns of paragraph indentation and runover lines are shown as two numbers separated by a hyphen. The first number is the paragraph indent and the second number is the runover, e.g., use 1-3 format for lists and 3-1 format for indented paragraphs.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.4.4</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">A <em>nested list,</em> a list within a list, has main entries and at least one level of subentries.<br>\n<br>\nThe main entry begins in cell 1. Each subentry level begins two cells to the right of the previous level. All runovers begin two cells to the right of the farthest indented subentry.<br>\n<br>\nTwo levels: 1-5, 3-5<br>\nThree levels: 1-7, 3-7, 5-7<br>\nFour levels: 1-9, 3-9, 5-9, 7-9\n\tetc.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.4.5</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><em>Displayed material</em> refers to text set off by blank lines and/or a different margin.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section"></div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">\n<h3>Example 1-1: Displayed Material (Print Only)</h3>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n \t<div class="sub-section"></div>\n<div class="sub-samples"><img src="images/01-01_example.jpg" alt="... the story begins with a line from poetry\n[The fields are awash in a sea of blue.]\nThe &#34;sea of blue&#34; refers to the wildflowers growing in abundance ..." />\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.4.5</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><em>Embedded material</em> refers to text inserted within a paragraph.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section"></div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">\n<h3>Example 1-2: Embedded Material (Print Only)</h3>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section"></div>\n<div class="sub-samples"><img src="images/01-02_example.jpg" alt="... the story begins with a line from poetry. [The fields are awash in a sea of blue.] The &#34;sea of blue&#34; refers to the wildflowers growing in abundance ..." /></div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t\t\n<div class="headerwrap"><a name="1.5" id="1.5"></a>\n<h2>1.5 &nbsp; &nbsp; Formatting for Magazines or Newsletters in Noninstructional Material</h2>\n</div>\n\n\t<div class="sub-section"></div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Where it is desirable to indicate ends of articles, stories, etc., a termination line consisting of a dot 5 followed by 12 consecutive dots 2-5 should be centered on a new line. Do not insert blank lines above or below this line unless required by other formats, e.g., headings, lists, poetry, etc. If there is insufficient room below the termination line for the heading and the first line of text, the new item should begin on a new page.</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\n<div class="headerwrap"><a name="1.6" id="1.6"></a>\n<h2>1.6 &nbsp; &nbsp; Volume Information</h2>\n</div>\n\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.6.1</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Volume Size.</strong> An agency may specify maximum braille volume size for the transcriptions that it sponsors. The decision may be based on the reading level, especially for lower grades. Volume size is dependent also on output as either single-side or interpoint.\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;End a braille volume with a logical break in content, e.g., at the end of a unit, part, chapter, or section.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;Adherence to this principle is more important than maintaining uniform volume size throughout the braille edition.</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.6.2</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><b>Volume Numbering.</b> Volumes are given consecutive arabic numbers that are to be:\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;Placed on all title pages.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;Indicated in the table of contents for the first volume. (See <em>Formats,</em> \xc2\xa72.10.3, Headings.)</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">c. &nbsp;Placed on the cover label. (See <em>Formats,</em> \xc2\xa71.17, Volume Labels.)</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.6.3</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Preliminary Volumes.</strong> A volume composed entirely of transcriber-generated pages, the table of contents, and other front matter is labeled as a \xe2\x80\x9cPreliminary Volume.\xe2\x80\x9d\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;The volume designation on the title page is \xe2\x80\x9cPreliminary Volume.\xe2\x80\x9d</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;An arabic number is added if there is more than one preliminary volume, i.e., \xe2\x80\x9cPreliminary Volume 2.\xe2\x80\x9d</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">c. &nbsp;An agency may designate \xe2\x80\x9cPreliminary Volume\xe2\x80\x9d as \xe2\x80\x9cVolume 1.\xe2\x80\x9d</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.6.4</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Supplemental Volumes.</strong> A volume composed entirely of segments of back matter (e.g., glossary, handbook, index, etc.) may be labeled as a supplement. This can be useful when the book is not transcribed from front to back and volume divisions are not known.\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;The volume designation on the title page is \xe2\x80\x9cSupplement\xe2\x80\x9d followed by the number of the supplement, e.g., \xe2\x80\x9cSupplement 2.\xe2\x80\x9d</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;The volume designation may be specific when the entire volume is composed of a single type of back matter. For example, \xe2\x80\x9cSupplement 1: Glossary.\xe2\x80\x9d</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.6.5</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>End of Volume. </strong>Indicate the end of each volume on the last page.\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;In each volume include a centered-and-numbered volume statement enclosed in transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s note indicators: End of Volume __.</p>\n    <p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;In the final volume include a centered end-of-book statement enclosed in transcriber\xe2\x80\x99s note indicators: The End.</p>\n    <p class="listcontrol">c. &nbsp;Whenever possible, precede the end-of-volume statement with a blank line.</p>\n\t<h3 class="list">Example 1-3: End-of-Volume Statement within Braille Page</h3>\n\n\t<div class="sub-samples-list">\n\t<p><span class="numbers">5</span><span class="Simbraille"> .1,9f]\xe2\xa0\x80,:y\xe2\xa0\x80d\xe2\xa0\x80sci5ti/s 2lieve t</span><br />\n\t<span class="numbers">6</span><span class="Simbraille"> mito*ondria\xe2\xa0\x80may\xe2\xa0\x80h\xe2\xa0\x80be5\xe2\xa0\x809vad]s ( e&gt;ly\xe2\xa0\x80</span><br />\n\t<span class="numbers">7</span><span class="Simbraille"> cells8</span><br />\n\t<span class="numbers">8</span><span class="Simbraille"> \xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80</span><br />\n\t<span class="numbers">9</span><span class="Simbraille"> \xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80\xe2\xa0\x80<span style="background:#ccc; capitalization.</p>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">e. &nbsp;If a title is too long to use as the running head, either adjust capitalization or choose key words from the title.</p>\n</div>\n<div The page number is implied.</p>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;Page numbers do not always appear on the first pages in the front matter. The page numbers are implied when the print page numbers start with something other than <strong>i</strong> or <strong>1</strong>.</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.11.6</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>No Print Page Numbers.</strong> Some books have no page numbers or there are sections of text without numbers. For example, a section of photographs with captions but without page numbers may be inserted between two consecutive page numbers. Sections at the end of the book also may be unnumbered.\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;Insert a row of unspaced dots 36 across the width of the line to indicate print page changes.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;Continue braille page numbering on these pages.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">c. &nbsp;If the page change indicator would fall on line 25, move it to the next braille page.</p>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">d. &nbsp;The page change indicator can be placed on line 1 if no running head is used.</p>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">e. &nbsp;If the page change indicator would fall at the top of a braille page, and a running head is used, place the page change indicator on line 2. No blank line is required between the running head and the page change indicator.</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.11.7</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Lettered Continuation Pages.</strong> Material on a print page typically continues on one or more braille pages.\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;Place the same print page number to end at the right margin on line 1 of each of these pages.</p>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;Precede the page number by an unspaced <strong>a</strong> for the first continued page, <strong>b</strong> for the second, etc. These letters are transcribed without the grade 1 indicator, and the usual three blank cells are left between the end of the text or running head and the lettered page number.</p>\n\t<h3 class="list">Example 1-11: Lettered Continuation Pages (Print Only)</h3>\n\t<div class="sub-samples-list"><img src="images/01-11_example.jpg" alt="Lettered Continuation Pages (Print Only) example" width="100%" /></div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\t<p class="listcontrol">c. &nbsp;Use double letters, <strong>aa</strong>, <strong>bb</strong>, etc., when the continuation page number goes beyond <strong>z</strong>.</p>\n</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n<div class="sub-section">1.11.8</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy"><strong>Combined Print Page Numbers.</strong> When the first page of text does not begin with a page numbered as <strong>i</strong>, <strong>I</strong>, or <strong>1</strong>, or has some other logical sequence of numbers, a combined print page number is inserted to account for the preceding pages. Do not combine different types of numbering systems, e.g., roman and arabic numerals.\n<p class="listcontrol">a. &nbsp;Combine the initial roman or arabic number with the number of the page on which the text section actually begins (e.g., <strong>i-v</strong>, <strong>I-V</strong>, or <strong>1-5</strong>) and place this combined page number at the right margin. If lettered continuation pages are required, they carry only the number of the page on which the text section actually occurs, e.g., <strong>av</strong>, <strong>aV</strong>, or <strong>a5</strong>. (See <em>Formats</em>, \xc2\xa72.3.8d, Print Page Information.)</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">b. &nbsp;When one or more pages contain only headings, such as the book title, part, unit, or chapter, combine the headings with the following text on a single braille page.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">c. &nbsp;Include the full pages of material omitted from the braille edition in a combined page number, e.g., <strong>25-29</strong>.</p>\n<p class="listcontrol">d. &nbsp;When a page is blank or has only an uncaptioned illustration, combine that/those page(s) with the next page on which text occurs. (See <a name="Sample-1.3-b" id="Sample-1.3-b"></a><a href="#Sample-1.3">Sample 1-3: Combined Print Page Numbers</a> on page 1-23.)</p>\n\t</div>\n<div class="clear"></div>\n\n<div class="headerwrap"><a name="1.12" id="1.12"></a>\n<h2>1.12 &nbsp; Print Page Numbers</h2>\n</div>\n\n\t<div class="sub-section">1.12.1</div>\n<div class="sub-section-copy">Material printed and read across two facing pages of a book, when there is no other material on either page, is treated as if it were on a single print page. The combined print page numbers (e.g., <strong>44-45</strong>) are placed to end at the right margin.
